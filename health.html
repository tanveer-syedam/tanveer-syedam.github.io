Healthcare AI Research

Over the last 25 years, my research has focused on medical imaging, multimodal AI, and clinical decision support systems spanning radiology, cardiology, oncology, and healthcare informatics. The work has evolved from early rule-based and statistical methods to deep learning and current multimodal and generative AI systems for diagnosis, reasoning, and clinical decision support.

Cardiovascular Image Analysis

The work in cardiovascular image analysis started during an exploratory project called AALIM at IBM in 2004. Anticipating the emergence of electronic health records, I developed the idea of patient similarity-guided decision support, where knowledge from past patients is used to guide diagnosis for new patients. This system integrated multimodal clinical data including ECG, echocardiography, CT, MRI, angiography, laboratory data, and clinical notes.

The system was deployed with Kaiser Permanente and Cedars-Sinai for clinical decision support and clinical trial recruitment. It supported over 1 million patients and demonstrated early success in multimodal clinical reasoning.

This work evolved from the Medical Sieve project into large-scale deep learning systems for chest X-ray interpretation, report generation, and clinical reasoning. The focus was on developing a structured understanding of radiology findings and generating clinically coherent reports from imaging data.
