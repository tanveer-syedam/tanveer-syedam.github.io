Research

My research spans computer vision, multimedia retrieval, healthcare AI, multimodal foundation models, semantic memory, and Verification AI systems for trustworthy intelligence.

Research Vision

Across multiple generations of AI, my work has focused on a central question:

How can we build intelligent systems that perceive, remember, reason, and verify before producing outputs that affect human decisions?

This has evolved from early work in vision and retrieval systems to modern foundation models and verification-centric AI architectures for high-stakes domains such as healthcare.

Flagship Direction: Verification AI

Verification AI for Trustworthy Clinical Systems

Verification AI represents a shift from generation-centric models to systems that can independently evaluate the correctness of AI outputs.

  • Fact-checking radiology report generation
  • Detecting hallucinations in multimodal clinical AI
  • Grounding findings in image evidence
  • Correcting generated reports before clinician review
  • Using verification signals for model training (preference optimization)

This work defines a new layer in AI systems: the verification layer.
