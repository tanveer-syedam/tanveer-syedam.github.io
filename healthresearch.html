Healthcare AI Research

Over the last 10+ years, my research has focused on building end-to-end Healthcare AI systems that integrate imaging, clinical, laboratory, and textual data for diagnostic reasoning, multimodal understanding, and decision support. These efforts span foundational algorithm development through large-scale translational deployment in clinical environments.

Multimodal Healthcare AI Platforms

A central thrust of my work has been the development of large-scale multimodal healthcare AI platforms that integrate electronic health records, imaging, laboratory data, and clinical narratives to enable patient-level reasoning and decision support.

Early work led to the development of the AALIM system, which introduced the concept of patient-similarity-driven clinical decision support. This system integrated multimodal patient records and enabled retrieval of clinically similar cases for diagnostic and treatment guidance.

Subsequent efforts expanded into the Medical Sieve project, which combined multimodal perception with knowledge-driven reasoning to support radiologists and cardiologists in complex diagnostic workflows. This work formed a foundation for cognitive imaging systems later incorporated into Watson Health Imaging products.

  • AALIM: Multimodal patient similarity and clinical decision support system (deployed in Kaiser Permanente and Cedars-Sinai)
  • Medical Sieve: Cognitive assistant for radiology and cardiology diagnosis
  • Watson Health Imaging: Translation of AI systems into clinical product pipelines

Clinical Decision Support Systems

A major focus has been the design of AI systems that assist clinicians in diagnostic reasoning by integrating multimodal evidence from imaging, text, and structured clinical data.

The AALIM system pioneered patient similarity-based reasoning, allowing clinicians to compare a patient’s multimodal profile with large cohorts of previously seen patients to support diagnosis and treatment decisions. This approach was later extended in Medical Sieve to incorporate knowledge-driven reasoning and structured report summarization.

  • Patient similarity-based diagnostic reasoning
  • Multimodal clinical summarization and evidence retrieval
  • Knowledge-driven diagnostic inference systems

Cardiovascular Imaging AI

Cardiovascular imaging has been one of the earliest and most extensive application areas, spanning echocardiography, CT, MRI, angiography, ECG, and auscultation signals.

Work in this area began with multimodal patient modeling and evolved into deep learning-based detection, segmentation, and disease classification systems used for clinical decision support and trial recruitment.

  • Echocardiography segmentation and anomaly detection
  • Cardiac CT calcium and dissection detection
  • Cardiac MRI segmentation and atlas-based modeling
  • Coronary angiography analysis and stenosis detection

Chest X-ray & Radiology AI

Chest X-ray AI research evolved from classification systems into generative and reasoning-based radiology models capable of report generation and clinical interpretation. The work spans fine-grained labeling, multimodal learning, and most recently, fact-checking of generated radiology reports.

Key Contributions

  • Multi-label disease classification in chest radiographs
  • Fine-grained clinical finding representation learning
  • Radiology report generation from imaging data
  • Knowledge-enhanced and reasoning-based AI systems
  • Fact-checking of generative radiology reports

Chest X-ray & Radiology AI

Chest X-ray analysis has been a major focus of recent work, particularly in developing deep learning systems for disease classification, report generation, and automated clinical reasoning.

This line of work evolved from structured finding detection models to generative AI systems capable of producing radiology reports, along with recent work on hallucination detection and fact-checking of generated clinical text.

  • Chest X-ray classification and multi-label disease detection
  • Radiology report generation using fine-grained label learning
  • Knowledge-enhanced learning for medical imaging AI
  • Fact-checking and verification of generative radiology reports

Brain Imaging AI

Research in brain imaging has focused on segmentation, registration, and lesion detection across modalities such as MRI, including contributions to benchmark challenges in neuroimaging.

Methods evolved from statistical modeling approaches to modern deep learning architectures and frequency-domain neural representations for robust 3D segmentation.

  • Brain tumor segmentation and lesion detection
  • Multi-atlas and learning-based registration methods
  • Fourier and frequency-domain neural architectures

Cancer & Multimodal Fusion

A major recent direction has been multimodal fusion for cancer and complex diseases, integrating imaging, genomics, pathology, and clinical data to model disease progression and treatment response.

This includes development of multiplexed graph neural networks and imaging-genomics association models, enabling prediction of outcomes and discovery of disease biomarkers.

  • Multimodal graph neural networks for outcome prediction
  • Imaging-genomics association discovery
  • Digital twin modeling for oncology and chronic disease

Interventional AI

Interventional imaging presents unique challenges requiring real-time, high-precision interpretation of medical images during procedures.

Work in this area includes AI systems for intravascular imaging, stent detection, and vessel segmentation, developed in collaboration with industry partners and translated into clinical guidance systems.

  • Intravascular ultrasound segmentation and analysis
  • Stent detection and malapposition analysis
  • Real-time procedural guidance algorithms

Healthcare Informatics

Healthcare informatics work has focused on large-scale population-level analytics, including comparative effectiveness research, comorbidity discovery, and predictive modeling from multimodal clinical datasets.

  • Population-level outcome modeling
  • Comorbidity and drug interaction discovery
  • Clinical data mining from longitudinal EHR systems
