Tanveer Syeda-Mahmood

IBM Fellow • AI Executive & Chief Scientist • Technology Strategist • Stanford Adjunct Professor

I build intelligent systems that perceive, reason, remember, search, and verify. My work spans computer vision, medical imaging, healthcare AI, multimodal foundation models, neuro-inspired architectures, and trustworthy AI systems deployed in real-world settings.

Over my career, I have led research and innovation initiatives that transformed advances in AI and machine learning into technologies, products, and businesses with significant impact. As an IBM Fellow, inventor, and technology executive, I work at the intersection of scientific discovery, organizational leadership, and technology strategy, helping shape the future of healthcare AI and intelligent systems.

I teach and mentor students in Stanford's Biomedical Data Science Program and collaborate with leading researchers, clinicians, startups, and institutions worldwide. My current interests focus on multimodal intelligence, memory-inspired AI, trustworthy generative systems, and the development of next-generation architectures that bring reasoning, knowledge, and learning closer together.
